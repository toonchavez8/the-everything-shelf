# Enchiladas Verdes
![](https://cdn.apartmenttherapy.info/image/upload/f_jpg,q_auto:eco,c_fill,g_auto,w_1500,ar_16:9/tk%2Fphoto%2F2026%2F06-2026%2F2026-06-Enchiladas-Verdes%2FEnchiladas-Verdes-104)

> These green enchiladas are cozy, saucy, and packed with tender chicken, all wrapped in tortillas and smothered in a tangy tomatillo salsa topped with salty cotija cheese.

> Source: https://www.thekitchn.com/enchiladas-verdes-recipe-23788188

## Ingredients

- 1 pound tomatillos, peeled and rinsed
- 2 medium serrano or jalapeño peppers, stemmed and seeded if desired
- 1 clove garlic, peeled
- 1/2 teaspoon chicken bouillon powder or paste, such as
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more as needed
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1/2 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced
- 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth
- 4 teaspoons vegetable oil, divided
- 12 (about 5-inch) corn tortillas
- 1 pound warm shredded, cooked chicken (4 cups, see recipe notes)
- 4 ounces Monterey Jack cheese, shredded (1 cup, if baking)
- Crumbled Cotija or queso fresco cheese
- Mexican crema or sour cream
- Sliced avocado
- Diced yellow onion
- Mexican rice
- Refried beans

## Steps

1. — Start the salsa verde: —
2. Place 1 pound peeled and rinsed tomatillos and 2 stemmed medium serrano peppers in a medium saucepan. Add enough cool water to cover the tomatillos by about 1-inch. Bring to a boil over medium heat. Reduce the heat to maintain a simmer and cook until the tomatillos are very soft and duller in color, 15 to 20 minutes.
3. Drain and transfer the tomatillos and peppers to a blender. Add 1 peeled garlic clove, 1/2 teaspoon chicken bouillon, 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, and 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin. Blend until smooth. Taste and season with more kosher salt as needed.
4. — Warm the tortillas: —
5. Heat 1 teaspoon of the vegetable oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat until shimmering. Swirl the skillet to coat the bottom in oil. Working in batches of 3 tortillas, place in an even layer in the skillet and heat until softened and pliable, about 1 minute per side. Stack on a sheet of aluminum foil and wrap the foil around the tortillas to keep them warm. Add 1 teaspoon of the vegetable oil to the skillet before each batch.
6. — Finish the salsa verde and assemble the enchiladas: —
7. Heat 1 tablespoon vegetable oil in the same skillet (no need to clean) until shimmering. Add 1/2 thinly sliced medium yellow onion and cook until softened and lightly browned in spots, about 4 minutes. Pour in the tomatillo sauce and 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Turn off the heat.
8. Assemble the enchiladas: Pick up 1 tortilla with tongs and dip it into the salsa verde, flipping it to coat. Place on a plate. Place a scant 1/4 cup warm cooked, shredded chicken across the center of the tortilla. Roll the tortilla up tightly into a cylinder. Place seam-side down in a single, tight layer in a 9x13-inch baking dish, covering them with foil as you go to keep them warm.
9. Option 1: To serve the enchiladas as is, transfer them to plates and top with the remaining warm salsa verde.
10. Option 2: To bake the enchiladas, keep them in the baking dish and pour the remaining salsa verde evenly over the top. Sprinkle with 4 ounces shredded Monterey Jack cheese and bake uncovered in a 350ºF oven until the cheese is melted, 15 to 20 minutes.
11. Serve with desired garnishes, refried beans, and Mexican rice.
12. — Recipe Notes —
13. Warming chicken: If you start with cold shredded chicken, heat it up with 2 to 3 tablespoons of the salsa verde in a medium nonstick skillet over medium heat until warmed through to keep the chicken from drying out.Make ahead: The salsa verde can be made up to 2 days ahead through Step 2. Refrigerate in an airtight container. Storage: Leftover enchiladas can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 3 days, but the tortillas will soften over time.
